History

White-water rafting has a rich history rooted in exploration and adventure. It began in the early 19th century as a practical way to navigate dangerous rivers for trade and exploration. One of the first recorded expeditions took place in 1842 when explorer John Fremont braved the Platte River using a rubber raft designed by Horace H. Day. These early journeys laid the foundation for what would later become a thrilling recreational activity.
